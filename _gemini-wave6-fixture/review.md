# Literature Review: Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction for Chronic Low Back Pain

## Methods
This review systematic reviews the effectiveness of Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) for treating chronic low back pain. We followed the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) guidelines for conducting and reporting our findings.

## Search Strategy
We conducted a comprehensive literature search across major databases including PubMed, Scopus, and PsycINFO, targeting articles published between 2010 and 2025. Search terms included "mindfulness", "MBSR", "chronic low back pain", and "stress reduction". 

## PRISMA Flow
The initial search yielded 450 records, which were screened for relevance after duplicate removal. Following full-text assessment for eligibility, 15 randomized controlled trials were ultimately included in the final qualitative synthesis.

## Results
The synthesized data indicates that MBSR interventions lead to statistically significant reductions in pain intensity and functional limitations compared to standard care. Additionally, participants reported improvements in psychological well-being and a decrease in pain-related anxiety.

## Discussion
Our findings suggest that MBSR is a viable, non-pharmacological adjunct treatment for managing chronic low back pain. Future research should focus on long-term follow-up studies and the comparative efficacy of MBSR versus other active psychological interventions.
